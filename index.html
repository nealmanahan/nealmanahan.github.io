About Me

I graduated from Columbia College with a degree in music and media management. Throughout my life performing as a musician and working various internships in Chicago music, I wasn’t sure how to find sustainability while maintaining the same infatuation with the art and industry. I was unsure about what path I ultimately wanted to follow, so I decided to pursue the opportunity to turn my college barista job into a career as a retail manager.

As a coffee shop manager, I loved looking at our sales and financial data for my shop. I spent hours analyzing it and using it to make decisions that elevated the store’s performance month after month, year over year. What I loved most though, was translating my findings to others throughout the organization. After nearly 5 years of honing my analytic skills and using them to make strategic decisions in coffee shops, I decided to make that the focus of my next career step. I came across an event covering “The Future of Listening”, a discussion on big data in the music industry presented by Sofar Sounds and General Assembly. This panel opened my eyes to the wider field of data analytics and more specifically, data science. Since then, I've completed General Assembly's Data Science Immersive gaining new technical skills and applying them to real-life use cases. I am excited to continue learning and look forward to bringing a unique perspective to a great opportunity!
